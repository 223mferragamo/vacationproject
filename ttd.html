THINGS TO DO

in Saint Martin

Saint Martin is a part of the Leeward Islands in the Caribbean Sea. All along the coast you can find beaches, resorts, and coves. There are lots of hiking trails, lakes, water falls, and other outdoor places to visit.



Beaches


If you plan on visiting Saint Martin, you must visit the incredible beaches there. Saint Martin has a total of 37 beaches. That's a lot to visit in one trip, so I'm going to tell you the best rated ones that are a must see.


Maho Beach

Maho Beach is famed as the spot to go if you like watching planes roar overhead, but it also offers snorkeling, swimming, watersports, and world-class beach bars. St. Maarten's western shore beach offers the excitement of planes flying overhead so low you'll feel like you can touch them. It's just next to the airport so planes take off and land just overhead.

In addition to the heart-pounding sight of low-flying huge aircraft, you can also enjoy a wide range of watersports from jet skiing to windsurfing and many others. It's also a prime spot for snorkeling to see a wide range of tropical fish. The beach bars here are also fantastic with unbelievable sunset views.

Orient Bay Beach

Orient Bay Beach on St. Maarten's northeastern shore is a well-known stretch of soft white sand offering watersports, relaxation, snorkeling, and pristine waters for wading and swimming. This beach is famed as the St. Tropez of the Caribbean and among the largest on the island. Located on the northwestern coast, it's ideal for a leisurely float or a snorkeling experience among the schools of sea life just offshore.

This beach offers tons of amenities from jet skiing and parasailing to beach volleyball courts, barbecues, and fresh seafood restaurants on the shore. You can also enjoy live music and shopping just steps away in the nearby village square.


Great Bay Beach

Great Bay Beach in Philipsburg offers a long stretch of gold sand, a mile-long boardwalk, and many amenities from shopping and dining to cocktails and relaxation. Shops just steps away from the beach allow you to find the ideal souvenir of your trip along with handmade arts and crafts by local artisans. You can head for one of the many seafood and barbecue restaurants along the shore, or of course, just spend the day swimming, splashing, or lying in the sand.

The boardwalk here is a mile long and offers tons of opportunities to soak up the local culture and atmosphere. You can also grab a relaxing seaside drink at a cocktail bar or café, and just recharge.

Mullet Bay Beach

Mullet Bay Beach offers an idyllic and serene place to relax away from the crowds while basking in the warm sun or palm-tree-shaded areas with local food and delicious drinks. This one is something of a hidden gem of the Sint Maarten side of the island, offering gorgeous sands and tons of great beachfront restaurants and bars but lower crowds than others. It's also one of several beaches where you can watch planes arriving and departing from the nearby airport.

If you're a golfer, this is the place to be since there is a nearby championship-level golf course. Whether you're here for the sun and sand, the restaurants and clubs, or sports, it's a great locale.


Simpson Bay Beach

Simpson Bay Beach skirts the runway of the Princess Juliana International Airport and offers an undeveloped strip of sand and bright turquoise waters, a quaint village, and natural rock formations. This is the place to come to wonder at planes taking off and landing, to take a dip in the waters that are so bright blue they seem to glow in the sun, and to wander the streets of a quiet, serene fishing village with independent small shops.

You can dine at small local restaurants serving up fresh seafood just caught, find that ideal souvenir, and catch photos of the Pointe Burgeaux rock formations. It's a serene escape into a picturesque tropical paradise.

Cupe Coy Beach

Cupecoy Beach offers a trio of small, secluded coves offering dramatic scenery, astounding limestone cliffs, and a relaxing and refreshing escape from the crowds of the surrounding towns. You can capture photographs of amazing natural formations or the islands offshore, kick back in a lounge chair on the golden sands and look out over the deep blue waters, and capture memories of fantastic sunset views on St. Maarten's southern shores.

This island is ideal for long walks in the sand, reading books, or taking a quick dip to cool off in the calm surf. A local beach bar offers fantastic cocktails, and you'll find memories you never forget.


Kim Sha Beach

Kim Sha Beach offers a lively atmosphere with watersports, beach bars and clubs, restaurants, outstanding sunsets, a vibrant nightlife scene, or good old-fashioned lounging in the sun. No matter what kind of beachgoer you are, this is an island that has something for everyone. Grab a lounger and kick back in the sand, go swimming in the water, or get out on the sea for surfing, jet skis, and other sports. After dark everything comes to life with a wild nightlife scene where the party never ends.

If you happen to be on the island's southwest edge, Kin Sha Beach has it all. You can dance the night away, catch spectacular sunset views, or just relax and be.

Friar's Bay Beach

Friar's Bay Beach offers a protected cove with calm surf and a destination for beach bums and culinary enthusiasts, offering fantastic Caribbean cuisine, azure seas, and stunning flora. This is a stretch of soft sand and clear water with a gorgeous backdrop and is lined with some of the best restaurants in the area. The calm waters here are ideal for swimming and relaxation since there aren't huge waves for watersports. If you're just in it for the relaxing beach experience, this is a great stretch.

For foodies, Friar's Bay offers up authentic Caribbean, jerk, and fusion eateries all along the beach. You can grab everything from takeaway to upscale sit-down just steps from the island's northwestern shore.


Little Bay Beach

Little Bay Beach is separated by a peninsula from its larger cousin and offers hotel resorts next to a stretch of undeveloped land with calm cove waters ideal for swimming. It's just minutes away from the historic Fort Amsterdam and is a spot that's outstanding for kids. You won't find kitesurfers or jet skiers here due to the shallow, calm waters, so this place is perfect for a long day of laying out in the sand and swimming in the bright blue Caribbean sea.

This beach on the island's southern side is great for a budget-friendly getaway or a luxurious, fully pampered experience. For the latter, choose one of the high-end resorts on one side of the beach.

Dawn Beach

Dawn Beach on the island's east side offers some of the most spectacular sunrise vistas on the island, as well as amazing snorkeling opportunities to see native sea life. The beach sits right on the border of Saint Martin and Sint Maarten, offering the best of both worlds. Many people come here to take in a romantic sunrise view after a night of partying, while others seek to snap photos for their Instagram feed.

If you're a snorkeling enthusiast, you'll also find some of the most exciting views on the island. Not only will you see colorful tropical fish, but you might even see exotic sea life such as squid zipping through the water.

Restaurants


These great restaurants in Saint Martin/Sint Maarten show off the rich culinary diversity of this small island. From French classics to slow-cooked BBQ to dishes with rich aromas from the use of Caribbean spices, Saint Martin is a melting pot of flavor. You won’t even need a table to try tangy Caribbean jerk BBQ from a roadside “Lolo” restaurant. When it’s time to relax, enjoy French fine dining and a bottle of wine with a view of the sea. Or, dine right on the sand at one of many beach bars serving fresh seafood and cocktails. Saint Martin may be small, but there are many great restaurants to explore. Here’s where to eat in Saint Martin and what to try.

The Greenhouse

The Greenhouse is a laidback beachside restaurant serving American comfort food. Fried prawns, mac and cheese, chicken wings – all the classics are here. And, you can enjoy fresh local fish cooked with plenty of cream, butter, and spices (the American way!).

The Greenhouse is located on beautiful Simpson Bay, looking out on Kim Sha Beach. Dine on the large patio in comfort, rain or shine, thanks to large beach umbrellas. Not hungry? Come for cold beer and cocktails with a view of the water. The Greenhouse has a full bar and a daily happy hour featuring discounted beverages.

Location: Simpson Bay, Sint Maarten

Open: Saturday–Thursday from 11 am to 9 pm, Friday from 11 am to 10 pm

Phone: +1 721-544-4173

Taloula Mango's

Taloula Mango’s is a hip bar and eatery on the Boardwalk in Philipsburg, Sint Marteen. Take a break from your stroll along the water to fuel up with burgers, ribs and chicken cooked the Creole way – broiled jerk-style and served with plenty of hot sauce.

There’s no need to settle for just 1 dish. Order from the tapas menu to try zesty Caribbean favorites and fusion food. The Caribbean conch fritters are a must-try – a fried cornmeal snack stuffed with local peppers. Be sure to stop by on Friday for DJs and beverages until late.

Location: Boardwalk, Philipsburg, Sint Maarten

Open: Monday–Thursday from 11 am to 4 pm, Friday from 11 am to 11 pm, Saturday from 11 am to 4 pm (closed on Sundays)

Phone: +1 721-526-0918

Océan 82

Océan 82 is a French restaurant specializing in fine dining and finer wine next to Grand Case-Espérance Airport’s landing strip in Saint Martin. It’s a great spot for a date night or special occasion, with views over the bay of Grand Case. Everything from the flavors to the presentation is impeccable, and the seascapes from your table are unrivaled.

The seafood takes the top spot at Océan 82. Fresh lobster dishes like broiled half lobster and lobster risotto make the most of the local catch. Fans of raw fish will enjoy fresh oysters and tuna tartar. For those who want something other than seafood, Océan 82 also prepares excellent French classics made with chicken and beef.

Location: 82 Boulevard de Grande Case Grand-Case, 97150, Saint Martin

Open: Daily from 3 pm to 10.30 pm

Phone: +590 690 77 77 57

Bistrot Caraibes

Bistrot Caraibes is an award-winning restaurant in Saint Martin that's dedicated to classic French cuisine. You won’t find many Caribbean spices here. Instead, you’ll be transported to Paris with dishes such as escargots, stuffed chicken breast (cordon bleu-style) and foie gras. Don’t worry – plenty of local seafood is incorporated into the menu. The broiled lobster is some of the best on the island.

Bistrot Caraibes offers a nice escape if you’re looking for an alternate to the local Caribbean spices and hot sauce you might have already experienced while on the island. You’ll still enjoy the fresh Caribbean sea breezes as the ocean is just a skip away from the restaurant.

Location: 81 Boulevard de Grand Case, Saint Martin 97150, Saint Martin

Open: Daily from 6pm to 10pm

Phone: +590 590 29 08 29

Restaurant L’Auberge Gourmande

Restaurant L’Auberge Gourmande serves creole cooking unlike any other among the rows of diners in Grand Case. The chef combines French inspiration and local favorites to create truly unique dishes. The jumbo Cajun prawns in mushroom sauce blend the flavors of the Caribbean Sea and the French countryside. The duck breast glazed with pineapple and lime juice adds a twist to a French classic.

Restaurant L’Auberge Gourmande is housed in one of the oldest traditional Caribbean villas on the island. Beautiful pillars and high ceilings make your evening even more elegant. It’s truly a one-of-a-kind dining experience in Saint Martin.

Location: Bd de Grande Case, Grand-Case 97150, Saint Martin

Open: Tuesday–Sunday from 6 pm to midnight

Phone: +590 590 87 73 37

Villa Royale

Villa Royale is a casual restaurant serving Creole classics like marinated whole snapper, goat curry, and fish court-bouillon. You won’t find white tablecloths here – this is homestyle cooking in a relaxed atmosphere.

Villa Royale’s homemade flavors have gained fame around the island. The large menu is full of marinated seafood and meat dishes made with traditional local recipes. Don’t forget to grab a cocktail or cold beer from the bar to wash down the spice. Villa Royale is ideal for large groups and families. The prices are very reasonable, and a kids’ menu and burger menu ensure everyone gets something they like.

Location: 47 Bd de Grande Case, Grand-Case 97150, Saint Martin

Open: Daily from 11 am to 11 pm

Phone: +590 590 87 07 42

Irie Gardens

Irie Gardens is a vegetarian and vegan restaurant in Sint Marteen where you can taste fresh fruit and vegetables prepared with local herbs and spices. Irie offers a welcomed escape from the meat-heavy dishes of most other local restaurants and is a haven for vegetarians.

Come for breakfast or lunch to enjoy fresh salads, sandwiches and stir-fried rice dishes. Want something a bit heavier? Irie Gardens prepares local fritters and fried plantains better than most. The dishes are made to order and well worth the wait. Not hungry? Grab a shake made with local tropical fruit to go.

Location: Front Street 155 Unit 8 (Walter Plantz Square), Philipsburg, Sint Maarten

Open: Monday–Saturday from 7.30 am to 5 pm

Phone: +1 721-543-3322

Caribbean Blend

Caribbean Blend is a beloved BBQ restaurant and open-air bar where you can find Creole classics prepared by broil masters. Take a break from the beach just across the street to tuck into huge racks of ribs, aromatic rice and beans, and plump prawns covered in the zesty house sauce.

After your meal, take a peek at the large open BBQ and watch as the broil master seasons and slow-cooks dishes to perfection. It’s a sight that embodies the Caribbean jerk tradition. Beers and cocktails are affordable at Caribbean Blend, which has made it a favorite spot to grab a drink and watch the waves.

Location: 99 Front St, Philipsburg, Sint Maarten

Phone: +1 721-520-9669

Sky's the Limit

Sky’s the Limit in Grand Case is a traditional “Lolo” restaurant serving Caribbean Jerk BBQ and beverages in the open. Lolos are open-air food stands and restaurants, and they are a big part of Saint Martin culture. You can’t miss out on a meal at a lolo while you’re here and Sky’s the Limit is regarded as one of the top ones on the island.

Take a seat at a picnic table and get ready to dig into huge portions of shrimp kabobs, pork chops, jerk chicken and local fish. Grab a bucket of beers to complete the experience. Lolos are very affordable and Sky’s the Limit is ideal for budget travelers who want to try real local cuisine.

Location: Bd de Grande Case, Grand-Case 97150, Saint Martin

Open: Tuesday–Sunday from 9 am to 10.30 pm

Phone: +590 690 35 67 84

Orange Fever

Orange Fever is a relaxed beach bar serving cold beers, cocktails and international food. Come for familiar favorites like burgers, Caesar salads, and curries. If you want something local, the fish tartar of the day and the broiled mahi-mahi are recommended picks.

Orange Fever has a roofed restaurant as well as setups with reclining chairs on the sand, so it’s a great place to mail up on the beach or take shelter from the sun. If you’ve ever dreamt of being served cocktails while sunbathing, this is the place to do it in Saint Martin.

Location: 222 Parc de la Baie Orientale Saint Martin, 97150, Saint Martin

Open: Daily from 9 am to 5 pm

Phone: +590 690 75 52 11
